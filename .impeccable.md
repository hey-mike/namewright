## Design Context

### Users
Solo founders and indie hackers, typically technical, making a high-stakes naming decision before launching. Using the tool during work hours, likely on a laptop. Impatient — want authoritative output fast. Will judge credibility of the research by how the interface looks.

### Brand Personality
Authoritative · Precise · Fast

The interface should feel like a premium research platform — like Perplexity or Bloomberg, not a creative brainstorming tool. The user is submitting a research brief and receiving a structured report. Every visual choice should reinforce: "this tool did real work."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: Perplexity.ai / Claude.ai light mode — clean, focused, lots of breathing room, research-output feel
- **Theme**: Light. Founders use this during the day for a credible business decision. Light reinforces trust and legibility.
- **Anti-reference**: Current design (Fraunces editorial warmth, JetBrains Mono labels, warm paper palette) — feels like a magazine, not a research tool. Too many competing visual elements.
- **Color**: Near-white base with barely perceptible cool tint. Single strong accent — deep indigo (NOT AI purple — more serious, research-grade). Clean near-black text.
- **Typography**: Bricolage Grotesque for display/headings (distinctive, authoritative, not on reject list). Geist Sans for body/UI text (clean, technical, precise).
- **Layout**: Single-column, focused. Max ~720px content width. Data-forward result cards. No decorative editorial elements.

### Design Principles
1. **Research, not brainstorm** — every screen should feel like a premium research platform, not a creative tool
2. **Data forward** — results are the product; lead with the finding, then the evidence
3. **One thing at a time** — intake feels like submitting a brief, not filling a form; results are scannable, not overwhelming
4. **Earn trust through precision** — tight spacing, consistent hierarchy, clean data tables signal that the research is real
5. **Speed signals** — loading states show what's happening (searching USPTO, checking domains) to build trust during the 20-40s wait
