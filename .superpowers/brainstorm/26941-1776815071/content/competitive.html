Competitive Landscape

Where each player sits on two axes: depth of research vs. speed/cost to the founder.

← Generic / Shallow Deep Research →

Generic + Expensive

Squadhelp — $299+ naming contests
Naming agencies — $5k–50k

Human-generated, slow, expensive. Not a direct threat.

Deep + Accessible ← TARGET

Namewright — $19, full trademark + domain research
Own this quadrant. No competitor here yet.

Generic + Free/Cheap

Namelix — free AI name generator, no research
ChatGPT — free, no structured output
Looka / Wix Name Generator — no TM data
High volume, no depth. Compete by showing them what they're missing.

Deep + Slow/Expensive

USPTO TESS — manual, free, overwhelming
Corsearch / Saegis — enterprise TM tools, $$$
IP attorneys — $500+/hr
What serious founders do today. Painful. Your real comparison.
Key insight: Namelix has the distribution but no research. USPTO has the research but terrible UX. You sit between them — credible research at instant, affordable scale. That gap is the business.
