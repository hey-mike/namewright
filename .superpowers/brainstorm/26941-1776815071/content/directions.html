Three Company Directions

Each is a coherent strategy, not just a feature list. Pick the shape that fits how you want to grow.

A

SEO Content Engine

Shape: Free tool + naming guides → organic traffic → paid conversion

How it grows: Write 20–30 articles targeting "startup name generator", "how to trademark a name", "best .io domain alternatives". Tool ranks. Strangers find you. Free partial report converts ~5–10% to paid.

Revenue timeline: 3–6 months to meaningful traffic. Slow start, strong compounding.

Moat: Content ages well. Hard to dislodge once ranked.

Risk: Google algorithm changes. Long feedback loop.

B

Community Launch Spike

Shape: Polish v1 → coordinated launch (Product Hunt + HN + Reddit) → testimonials → iterate

How it grows: One big launch day drives 500–2000 visitors. Convert 2–5% to paid. Use social proof to seed communities (Indie Hackers, r/startups, startup Discord servers). Repeat with feature launches.

Revenue timeline: Revenue on day 1. Spike fades — requires ongoing content or repeat launches.

Moat: Reputation + testimonials. First-mover in the niche.

Risk: Launch momentum is fragile. Hard to sustain without audience.

C

Partner Distribution

Shape: Embed in existing founder tools → borrow their audience → grow via referrals

How it grows: Strike deals with startup newsletters (The Hustle, Indie Hackers, Starter Story), accelerator onboarding flows, and no-code tool ecosystems (Webflow, Bubble marketplaces). They promote, you split or pay flat.

Revenue timeline: Fast if a partner says yes. Dependent on others' timelines.

Moat: Exclusive partnerships. Network effects if embedded in high-traffic tools.

Risk: Partners have leverage. Single point of failure if a partner churns.
